Here's what you missed — the attack's tell-tale signs

01

Sender domain mismatch

The email came from security-noreply@cybershield-llc.com — not from Google or Microsoft. Legitimate security alerts always come from the actual company's domain.

02

Artificial urgency

"Verify within 24 hours" and "unusual activity detected" are classic social engineering pressure tactics. Urgency overrides your critical thinking. Real providers rarely threaten immediate account lockout.

03

URL didn't match the platform

The login page URL was aegis.cybershield-llc.com/sim/... — not accounts.google.com or login.microsoftonline.com. Always check the address bar before entering credentials.

04

No MFA prompt

Real logins from Microsoft and Google trigger multi-factor authentication. If an "account verification" page accepts your password without sending a code to your phone, it's likely a phishing page.

What to do when you see the real thing

🚫 Don't click

When an email creates urgency around account access, go directly to the site by typing the URL — never click the link in the email.

🔍 Check the sender

Hover over the From address. The display name can say anything — the actual sending domain is what matters.

🔒 Verify the URL

Before entering any credentials, confirm the URL is the exact domain you expect. Typosquatting is common: microsofft.com, g00gle.com.

📢 Report it

Forward suspicious emails to your IT or security team. Even if you're unsure, report it — false positives are far cheaper than breaches.
